CAFE NEUROTICO: CREMA - User Manual

CAFE NEUROTICO: CREMA

1. INTRODUCTION: THE SWEET FOAM ON TOP

YOUR LIBRARY. ELEVATED. FULLSCREEN. CONTROLLER-READY.

Crema is the reddish-brown, frothy foam layer that sits on top of a freshly brewed espresso. It consists of water, coffee oils, and pressurized CO2 bubbles created during high-pressure extraction. It signifies freshness and proper pressure.

If CNGM (Cafe Neurotico Game Manager) is the robust espresso machine handling the intense pressure of scraping, managing, and organizing your data, CREMA is the beautiful result you actually consume.

CREMA is designed from the ground up to be a 1080p, controller-first, fullscreen interface. It abandons mouse cursors and complex text entry for a pure console-like experience. You sit back on the couch, grab your gamepad, listen to some lo-fi or your own custom music, and just play.

2. LIBRARY INTEGRATION & THE CNGM BOND

CREMA and CNGM are two sides of the exact same coin. They share the same GameManagerConfig folder and the exact same games.db database file.

CRITICAL TIP: DATA MANAGEMENT

While CREMA has built-in scraping capabilities to grab covers and trailers, it is highly recommended to do your heavy-lifting data entry (importing libraries, syncing Heroic, syncing Steam, typing descriptions) using the CNGM Desktop App.

CNGM is built for a mouse and keyboard. CREMA is built for a gamepad. Let each app do what it does best.

Heroic Launcher Configuration

To ensure CREMA functions seamlessly like a real console, you don't want the Heroic Games Launcher window popping up every time you launch an Epic or GOG game.

To fix this: Open your Heroic Launcher, go to Settings -> General, and ensure "Minimize Heroic on Game Launch" or "Hide to Tray" is enabled. This guarantees Heroic stays invisible while your game plays, and CREMA takes the spotlight back when you exit.

3. CONTROLS & NAVIGATION

CREMA operates on a unified controller mapping system. You can change the visual icons (Xbox, PlayStation, Nintendo) in the System Menu to match what you are holding.

  • D-PAD / LEFT STICK: Navigate menus and game lists.
  • L1 / R1 (Bumpers): Page Jump (Skips 10 games at a time).
  • SOUTH BUTTON (A / Cross / B): Select / Play Game / Confirm.
  • EAST BUTTON (B / Circle / A): Back / Cancel / Exit Menu.
  • START / OPTIONS: Open the System Menu.
  • SELECT / SHARE: Open Game Options (Scraping, Favorites, Trailers).
  • NORTH BUTTON (Y / Triangle / X): Global Search (Opens On-Screen Keyboard).
  • WEST BUTTON (X / Square / Y): Toggle between Cover Art and Trailer/Screenshot in the main view.
  • L3 / R3 (Stick Clicks): Jukebox Controls (Next Track / Restart Track).

4. MEDIA & SCRAPING ENGINE

If you add a game but it's missing artwork or metadata, press SELECT on the game to open the Game Options, and choose SCRAPING ENGINE.

Available Scraping Tools:

  • SCRAPE ALL (DEFAULT): The fastest method. Connects to Steam, searches the game title, and automatically downloads the Cover Art, 5 Screenshots, Genre, Release Year, Developer, and Description in seconds.
  • CUSTOM COVER (STEAMGRIDDB): Sometimes official art is boring. If you have added your free SteamGridDB API key, this will open a gallery of high-quality custom covers. Use the D-Pad to pick your favorite.

Tip: If the automatic scraper grabs the wrong game (e.g., a sequel instead of the original), select REFINE SEARCH to manually type the exact title using the on-screen keyboard.

5. THE JUKEBOX

CREMA features a robust, fully-integrated music player. Access it from the main System Menu.

Adding Custom Music

To build your collection, go to the folder where the CREMA app is located and add your audio files (MP3, WAV, OGG, FLAC) to the newly created folder named: CREMA_CUSTOM_MUSIC.

Close and reopen the Jukebox for the app to scan the ID3 metadata (Artist, Album, Title, Cover Art) of your files.

Jukebox Features:

  • Browse by Categories: Sort your library by All Songs, Artists, or Albums.
  • Playlists: Create custom playlists using the On-Screen Keyboard. Select any song or album to add it directly to a playlist.
  • Fullscreen Mode: Press the WEST BUTTON (X/Square) while in the Jukebox to enter an immersive, animated fullscreen player.
  • Background Audio: Jukebox music continues playing seamlessly while you browse your game library! Use L3/R3 to skip tracks without opening the menu.

6. SCREENSAVER & SLEEP MODE

The Screensaver

If you step away from your controller, CREMA will eventually trigger its dynamic screensaver (configurable between 1 to 30 minutes in Settings). The screensaver has two modes:

  • Screenshots: Slowly pans and zooms across high-res screenshots from your library.
  • Trailers: Plays randomized gameplay trailers you have downloaded.

While the screensaver is active, you can press a button to instantly Launch the displayed game, or add it to your Favorites/Want-to-Play list without leaving the screensaver.

Sleep Mode & Waking Up

When you launch a game, CREMA fades to black and enters "Sleep Mode," yielding all system resources to your game. Once you are finished playing and close the game, you need to "Wake Up" CREMA.

By default, pressing START + SELECT simultaneously on your controller will violently yank CREMA back to the absolute front of your screen, burying any lingering launchers (like Steam or Heroic) and restoring the background music.

(You can change this combo in the System Menu -> Wake Up Method to prevent accidental triggers).

7. SETTINGS & AUDIO

Press START anywhere to access the System Menu.

  • Color Scheme: Instantly switch between dozens of themes ranging from modern gradients to retro console aesthetics.
  • Sound Settings: Adjust master volume, toggle UI sound effects, and choose your Background Music (BGM).

BGM Modes

If you don't want to use the Custom Music folder, CREMA comes with built-in, looping background tracks composed by Schwarzenegger Belonio (Migfus20) to set the mood of your console. Choose between Jazz, Lo-Fi, Ambient, or Piano.
